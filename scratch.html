My life in 4-H and FFA

I began participating in 4-H when I was about 8 years-old. It soon became a very large part of my life, and would reamin as one for the next 10 years. The summer before my freshman year of High School, I was able to begin participating in FFA, and continued all 4 years of High School.

Species I have shown:

  1. Sheep
    • Dorsets

    • Suffolks

  2. Rabbits
    • New Zealands

    • Britannia Petites

    • English Spots

    • Checkered Giants

  3. Beef Cattle
    • Herefords

While I have personaly shown/owned all of these specices and breeds, I have shown nearly every specices eligible to be shown under 4-H and FFA guidlines through a compeition known as Round Robin.

While showing in 4-H and FFA, there are two main compeitions you compete in. The first is Showmanship. Showmanship is all about the invidual showing the animal and how well they can show off the animal. Depending on the species being showm, showmanship will either be knowledge based, or handling based. The second compeition is Type. Type is all about the animal and how well it fits a breed standard set by different organizations.

While beef cattle are my favorite to show in showmanship, rabbits are my favorite to show in type. I have worked for 6 years breeding, selecting and showing a competitive line of New Zelands, with an emphasis on the blue variety.

I show both rabbits at the local level, and the national level at shows sactioned by the American Rabbit Breeders Association (ARBA), which sets the breed standards for each recognized breed.
